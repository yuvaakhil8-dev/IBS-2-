AI-Driven Tuberculosis
PPI Prediction Platform

An advanced end-to-end bioinformatics pipeline utilizing Siamese CNNs, 170-dimensional feature extraction, and SHAP-based Explainable AI to predict protein-protein interactions in Mycobacterium tuberculosis H37Rv.

Deep Learning Bioinformatics Explainable AI Siamese CNN

The Challenge

Tuberculosis is driven by thousands of protein interactions controlling survival, drug resistance, and pathogenicity. Experimentally identifying these interactions is:

  • Expensive and resource-intensive
  • Highly time-consuming
  • Requires extensive wet-lab experiments

Our AI Solution

We replace slow experimental methods with an intelligent computational pipeline that accurately predicts interactions and provides deep biological context.

  • Cost-effective computational screening
  • Rapid prediction using Siamese Neural Networks
  • Biological explainability using SHAP

End-to-End Architecture

From raw FASTA sequences to interactive dashboards, our platform goes far beyond simple classification.

01

Dataset & Balancing

Sourced from STRING Database (Taxonomy 83332). Perfectly balanced dataset of 4,000 interaction pairs (2,000 positive, 2,000 negative) with strict leakage checks.

02

Feature Extraction

Generated a rich 170-dimensional feature vector per protein encompassing Amino Acid Composition, Dipeptide patterns, and Physicochemical properties.

03

Siamese CNN

Dual CNN branches encode proteins into 128-dimensional embeddings, extracting sequence motifs automatically before concatenation and sigmoid classification.

04

Explainable AI

Integrated SHAP to explain why the model predicted an interaction, identifying critical residue hotspots, binding regions, and generating contact map overlays.
